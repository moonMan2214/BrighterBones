Company History

Brighter Bones was established in 2002 as the public face of regenerative bone medicine. Operations ceased in 2003 following unexpected challenges in our late-stage trials.

Our Backstory

New Horizons Therapeutics was founded in 1990 in the Soviet Union in the years following the Chernobyl disaster. Inspired by the need to treat radiation-induced bone damage, our scientists developed revolutionary regenerative treatments that combined advanced stem cell therapy with precision radiation targeting.

Our goal was clear: create a cure for bone cancer and radiation-related illnesses. For many patients, we offered real hope of recovery and a brighter future.
OUR SHUTDOWN

During the last phases of testing in 2002, something went horribly wrong. Subjects began exhibiting severe, unexplained side effects. This led to the immediate shutdown of New Horizons.

After the incident, Brighter Bones was born — continuing the mission with renewed determination.

Personal Statement - Kevin Hart

Hello. I'm Kevin Hart. I’ll be telling the story of what I know happened in the New Horizons company. Hopefully this doesn't get in the wrong hands.

Well to start this off I was trying to get into a science company after college. It was a bumpy ride but eventually I got hired at New Horizons for bone cancer and radiation medicine and cures. They always seemed so strange when I first worked there, like sometimes I would randomly be kicked out of ‘important meetings’ it just never made sense.

For my first year I didn’t do much. I only stayed because they made me. Long story short, I got roped in through threats to my family and lots of money being bribed. I regret ever signing that contract…

In 1991 their first subject arrived. I never got to find out his name, but he was an older man who was a war veteran from world war 2. He was always calm until we tested him. New Horizons always claimed to be a good ethical company… but they weren't. If you would have seen the things they did to that man… You would have been so doomed.

This man grew in inhuman ways. He was kept in a cold light room, but we ended up taking the lights out because he hated it. Day after day he morphed into a creature who was tall and shaky. He always wanted to be called Grunkle Doodley. I always thought it was because he was crazy. For some reason they sent him home. He couldn’t live on his own and started morphing and being remade in inhuman ways in the darkness. He was sent with employees to watch him and make sure the lights stayed on at all times.

Years later after patient 7 escaped (more on him later) someone forgot to keep one of the lights on and… he tortured the employees till they were dead. After that no one knows where he went.

But in between those times there were more people. Every time they would treat their lab rats they would die in horrible ways. Some would grow so till that their bones would snap, others would grow extra skin till they couldn't breathe. It was bad.

Although many people died, one final subject arrived. Patient 7. I didn't know his name but one of my co-workers did. It's a little hazy but I think his first name was… Carson. He was different from the others. Once he took the treatment he grew… So very tall but didn't die. He mutated into a giant thing. He grew tiny gills and his nose grew bigger. He had an attraction to… feet. When we would feed him he would only eat the feet or wouldn't eat at all if there were no feet. So we stopped feeding him. He mutated even more screaming and begging for feet. It never worked. He mutated so much into a giant creature and almost escaped. We had to keep the lights on at all times. He was kept there for months.

One windy day, 3/17/02 the power went out. I was not there that day but the next day everyone was dead. Everybody had lost their feet, completely off. I can't even explain what went through my mind that day. I called the police and they arrived. They didn't even know what to do. The police couldn’t even check the cameras because the creature broke them. That day we shut down. I checked police reports days later and they died of some sort of radioactive poison. From my personal guess is that it somehow gained a way to inject poison.

After that incident the company was rebranded to Brighter Bones. Nothing really happened there. It was a reminder of that fateful day.
